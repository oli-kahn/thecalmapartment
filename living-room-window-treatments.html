8 No-Drill Window Treatments for Rental Living Rooms

Cover your windows without putting a single hole in the wall. Your deposit stays whole.

Rental windows usually come with one of two things: ugly vertical blinds you're not allowed to remove, or nothing at all. Either way, drilling brackets into the wall or frame is off the table. These eight window treatments work around every landlord restriction. They tension-fit, stick on, snap on with magnets, or hang from what's already there. Every single one comes off clean when you move.

01

Make a DIY roman shade with a tension rod and fabric

Fold a piece of fabric over a small tension rod inside the window frame, secure the hem with iron-on hemming tape, and you have a roman shade that cost about twenty dollars and took thirty minutes. No sewing machine, no brackets, no tools beyond an iron. Pick a medium-weight cotton or linen-blend fabric — anything too thin looks limp, anything too heavy won't fold cleanly when you push it up. To raise the shade, just accordion-fold the fabric upward and tuck it behind the rod. Search for "tension rod roman shade DIY" on YouTube for a visual walkthrough; the fabric and hemming tape you can grab at any craft store.

Tension rod twists into place with rubber ends. Removes in ten seconds. The window frame is untouched.
02

Clip lightweight fabric shades with adhesive blind clips

Adhesive blind clips are small plastic brackets with sticky backing that stick directly onto the inside of your window frame. You clip a lightweight fabric shade into them — a flat panel of cotton or a pre-made paper blind — and they hold it in place without any hardware touching the wall. Each clip supports about half a pound, so this works best with sheer curtains or lightweight cotton panels, not heavy blackout fabric. Clean the frame surface with rubbing alcohol first and let the adhesive set for an hour before clipping anything in. The clips peel off when heated with a hairdryer. Search Amazon for "adhesive blind clips" to find packs of six or eight.

Sticks inside the frame, not on the wall. Heat removes them clean. Zero landlord evidence.
03

Snap magnetic blinds onto metal window frames

If your apartment has metal window frames — common in older buildings and high-rises — magnetic blind brackets snap right onto the steel surface and hold a roller or cellular shade. The magnets are strong enough that you can tug the blind up and down daily without dislodging them. Check whether your frame is actually magnetic with a fridge magnet before ordering anything; aluminum frames look like steel but won't work. Search for "magnetic blinds for metal windows" and look for brackets with a rubberized back so they don't scratch the frame finish.

Magnetic attachment only. Pulls off by hand. The frame finish stays intact.
04

Apply peel-and-stick frosted window film

Frosted window film gives you full privacy while letting daylight through — no curtain hardware required. You apply it with water and a squeegee; it clings to the glass without any adhesive touching the frame or wall. This is the fix for ground-floor apartments where pedestrians walk past at eye level, or for windows facing a neighboring building six feet away. Measure your window pane, cut the film about half an inch larger on each side, spray the glass with water, smooth the film on, and trim the excess with a utility knife. When your lease ends, peel it off in one sheet — no residue, no scraping. Search Amazon for "frosted window film privacy" and pick a pattern you can live with every day.

Static cling or low-tack adhesive on glass only. Removes in one piece. Walls and frames untouched.
05

Install cordless cellular shades that tension-fit inside the frame

Cordless cellular shades — also called honeycomb shades — are designed to fit inside a window frame with spring tension, no brackets or screws. You press them into place and the built-in tension mechanism holds them against the sides of the frame. They give you real blackout or light-filtering coverage that looks like custom blinds, and the cordless design means no dangling strings (which is also the safer option if you have kids or cats). Measure the inside width of your frame to the nearest eighth of an inch — these shades are cut to order and a quarter-inch mistake means they won't fit. Search for "cordless cellular shades tension fit" and order from a retailer that lets you input exact measurements.

Spring tension holds them inside the frame. No screws, no wall contact. Pops out when you move.
06

Hang bamboo roll-up blinds with over-door hooks or a tension rod

Bamboo roll-up blinds add warmth and texture to a rental window without permanent hardware. Two mounting methods work: hang them from a tension rod set inside the frame, or use over-door hooks if your window has a top ledge the hooks can rest on. The blinds themselves come with a cord and pulley mechanism that lets you roll them up to different heights during the day. Bamboo blinds filter light softly — they won't black out a room but they give you daytime privacy while casting a warm, natural glow. Search for "bamboo roll-up blinds cordless" and grab a matching tension rod sized to your frame width.

Hangs from a tension rod or hooks. Nothing screws into anything. Takes five minutes to set up or take down.
07

Hide landlord blinds with a fabric valance on Command hooks

If your rental came with ugly vertical blinds or cheap aluminum minis that you're not allowed to take down, a fabric valance mounted above them hides the header rail and softens the whole window. Use two or three small Command hooks above the window, clip a lightweight valance onto them, and the landlord's blinds disappear behind a panel of fabric. A valance only needs to be about 14 to 18 inches deep to cover the rail; pick a linen or cotton blend in a solid color that matches your walls. The Command hooks peel off clean when heated. Search for "clip-on valance" or buy a fabric panel and attach clip rings yourself.

Command hooks remove without paint damage. The landlord's blinds stay intact underneath. You just hid them.
08

Use no-drill curtain rod brackets like 3M Claw or Kwik-Hang

No-drill curtain rod brackets let you hang real curtain rods — and therefore real curtains — above your window without touching a drill. 3M Claw brackets use a drywall-gripping mechanism that holds up to 15 pounds; Kwik-Hang brackets hook onto the top edge of your window trim and support up to 20 pounds. Both install in minutes and leave no holes behind. The tradeoff is weight capacity — they won't hold heavy velvet drapes, but they handle standard cotton or linen curtain panels without issue. Make sure your window has trim for Kwik-Hang (it needs a ledge to hook onto), or use 3M Claw if you have bare drywall above the frame. Search Amazon for "3M Claw curtain rod brackets" or "Kwik-Hang curtain rod brackets" to compare both options.

No-drill installation. 3M Claw pulls out of drywall clean. Kwik-Hang lifts off the trim. No spackle, no paint touch-ups.
