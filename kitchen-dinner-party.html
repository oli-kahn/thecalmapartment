8 Kitchen Upgrades Under $20 That Make Hosting Dinner Parties Possible in a Rental

Feed eight people without your kitchen fighting back. And keep your deposit on the way out.

Rental kitchens are not built for dinner parties. You get one narrow counter, a single overhead light that makes everything look like a gas station bathroom, and a landlord who said no to mounting anything. These eight fixes cost under twenty bucks each and none of them need a drill. They just make the space work when you have people over.

← Back to all rooms
01

Stick adhesive hooks under your upper cabinets for serving utensils

Heavy-duty adhesive hooks stuck to the underside of your upper cabinets put ladles, tongs, and serving spoons right above the counter where you need them. During a dinner party you grab a serving spoon without digging through a drawer mid-course. Get hooks rated for at least 3 pounds each and space them about 4 inches apart so utensils don't knock into each other. Clean the underside of the cabinet with rubbing alcohol before sticking the hooks on. Greasy surfaces will make them fall, and a falling hook with a metal ladle on it during a party is not the vibe you want.

Peels off clean when heated with a hairdryer. The cabinet underside goes back to normal.
02

Get a set of stackable serving trays and platters

A set of stackable serving trays lets you put appetizers, bread, sides, and desserts on the table without occupying every square inch of counter. When the party's over they nest into each other and slide into a single cabinet. Look for a set with three sizes so the largest holds a main dish and the smallest handles olives or nuts. Melamine or bamboo trays are lighter than ceramic and less likely to chip when guests pass them around. No mounting, no drilling, no hardware. They just stack and live in a cabinet until the next dinner.

Freestanding items. They move with you to every apartment you'll ever rent.
03

Put peel-and-stick LED lights under your cabinets

Battery-powered LED light bars with peel-and-stick backing attach to the underside of upper cabinets in about thirty seconds. The warm light makes a rental kitchen feel intentional instead of that one fluorescent ceiling fixture washing everything out. Most under-cabinet LED bars run on AA or AAA batteries and last through several dinner parties before needing a swap. Some come with a remote that dims them. For hosting, warm white light in the 2700K to 3000K range makes food and people look better than cool white. Stick them near the front edge of the cabinet so the light hits the counter, not the backsplash.

Peels off without residue when the lease ends. Bring them to the next kitchen.
04

Span a tension rod between cabinets for dish towels and oven mitts

A small tension rod wedged between two upper cabinets, or across the opening under a lower cabinet, gives you a rail for hanging dish towels, oven mitts, and aprons from S-hooks. During a dinner party you pull a towel without opening a drawer with flour on your hands. Most slim tension rods extend from 16 to 28 inches and hold a few pounds of fabric. Twist it tight so it doesn't slip when you yank a towel off mid-cooking. The rubber end caps grip without scratching the cabinet sides. If your cabinets have a gap between them that's wider than the rod extends, put it inside a lower cabinet opening instead.

Twists in, twists out. The cabinets stay untouched underneath the rubber caps.
05

Mount an adhesive spice rack on the side of a cabinet or the backsplash

A slim adhesive spice shelf sticks to the side of a cabinet box or an empty stretch of backsplash tile. It gets spices off the counter and within arm's reach while you cook, which is the difference between seasoning as you go and forgetting the paprika entirely. Most adhesive spice racks hold six to eight standard jars and the adhesive pads cure fully in 24 hours. Clean the surface with alcohol first. Tile and smooth painted cabinet sides hold best. Textured or porous surfaces will eventually let go, and nobody wants twelve spice jars hitting the floor during a dinner party.

Adhesive releases with heat. The tile or cabinet side is untouched underneath.
06

Stick a magnetic knife bar to the backsplash with adhesive backing

An adhesive-backed magnetic knife bar on the backsplash holds your chef's knife, paring knife, and bread knife right on the wall. It frees a drawer and makes the kitchen look like someone actually cooks in it instead of just microwaving leftovers. The strong magnets grip blades through the stainless steel face, and the whole bar sticks to the wall with heavy-duty adhesive strips. Let the adhesive cure overnight before hanging knives on it. A 12-inch bar holds three to four knives comfortably. If your backsplash is textured or uneven, stick it to the smooth side of a cabinet box instead.

Adhesive removes clean with heat. The wall underneath is undamaged.
07

Hook an over-cabinet door basket inside a cabinet for wraps and napkins

A wire or plastic basket that hooks over the inside of a cabinet door gives you a hidden spot for aluminum foil, plastic wrap, ziplock bags, and cloth napkins. During a dinner party you restock napkins or grab foil for leftovers without anyone seeing the mess behind the door. The basket hangs from two metal brackets that rest over the top of the door. Measure the gap between your cabinet door and the shelf inside before buying. Most over-door baskets need about half an inch of clearance for the door to close fully. Lifts off in one second on move-out day.

Hangs from the door edge. No screws, no adhesive touching the cabinet face.
08

Stick an adhesive paper towel holder under a cabinet

An adhesive paper towel holder mounted under an upper cabinet keeps the roll off the counter where it always gets knocked over or wet. During a party someone spills red wine on the counter and you grab a paper towel in one motion instead of hunting for the roll that rolled behind the toaster. Most adhesive holders fit standard 11-inch rolls and the mounting plate sticks to the flat underside of a cabinet. Let it cure for a full day before loading a roll on it. Position it near the sink but not directly above it. Steam from hot water loosens adhesive over time.

Adhesive plate peels off with heat. The cabinet underside stays as it was.
