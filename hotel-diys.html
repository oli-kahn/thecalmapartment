8 Hotel DIYs You Can Actually Do in a Rental

No walls were harmed in the making of this guide.

Hotels use a handful of repeatable tricks to make a room feel considered without remodeling anything: a tray that corrals loose items, lampshades that add texture, pillows layered in mixed patterns. These eight DIYs borrow those same moves and adapt them for a rental. Every idea works on furniture you own, surfaces you can protect, or pieces you can swap back before the landlord walks through. The walls stay exactly as you found them.

01

Paint Stripes on Thrifted Furniture

Pick up a dresser or side table from Facebook Marketplace or a thrift store, then use painter's tape to mark off stripes across the drawers or top panel. A quart of paint in a single contrasting color covers a standard dresser. Paint inside the tape lines, let it dry, peel the tape, and you have a piece that reads as custom without touching a single wall in your rental. Matte or chalk-finish paint adheres to most existing finishes without sanding. The entire project lives on furniture you own and can take with you.

Zero wall contact. Paint goes on a piece of furniture you own, not on the rental. Strip it or repaint it anytime.
02

Stick Graphic Tape Borders Around Trim

Peel-and-stick graphic tapes from Etsy come in patterns ranging from geometric lines to floral borders. Run them along window casings, doorframes, or around a plain mirror to add definition where the rental trim is uninspired. The tape presses into place by hand and peels off cleanly when you are done. No hairdryer, no solvent, no adhesive residue on the trim. A 33-foot roll covers roughly two doorframes. The trim underneath looks exactly like it did before.

Peel-and-stick tape removes cleanly from painted trim. No paint, no residue, no trace on move-out day.
03

Add a Marble Top With Adhesive

Find a marble remnant at a stone yard or architectural salvage shop. Many sell offcuts for under twenty dollars. Attach it to a thrifted side table or nightstand using removable adhesive strips rated for the weight of the stone. The marble adds a cool, heavy surface that feels expensive, and the strips create a barrier between the stone and the wood underneath. The original tabletop stays scratch-free. When you move, the strips release cleanly and the marble lifts off. The table goes back to its original state.

Adhesive strips release cleanly from the wood surface. The original table stays intact. Pops off for moving day.
04

Layer Throw Pillows Like a Hotel

Hotels rarely use matching pillow sets. They mix stripes, florals, and a solid or plaid in the same color family, and the combination reads as curated rather than chaotic. Start with two pillows in a neutral base, add one in a stripe, and one in a small-scale floral or botanical print. Buy covers only, not whole pillows, so you can swap them out when your taste changes without storing bulky inserts. The pillow covers slide onto whatever pillows you already have on the sofa or bed. No commitment, no damage, no tools.

Pillow covers slide onto pillows you already own. No modification to furniture, walls, or anything the landlord cares about.
05

Thread One Color Through the Room

Pick one signature color: forest green, dusty blue, or buttery yellow. Repeat it in four or five places around the room. Pillow covers, a throw blanket draped over a chair, a ceramic vase on a shelf, and a framed print that includes that color. The repetition makes the room feel connected and intentional without matching sets. Every item is a freestanding object you pack into a box on moving day. None of them attach to the rental. The color leaves with you when the lease ends.

Decor-only. Every item is a freestanding object you pack into a box. Nothing attaches to the rental.
06

Hang Removable Wall Murals

Peel-and-stick wallpaper or fabric panels create a headboard effect behind the bed without a single nail or screw. Position a panel the width of your bed frame and about four feet tall behind the pillows. It reads as a structured backdrop. Rental-grade adhesive backing holds on painted drywall and removes in one piece when warmed with a hairdryer on low heat. Fabric panels have the added benefit of softening sound in a small bedroom. The wall underneath stays clean and unpunctured.

Rental-grade peel-and-stick adhesive. Removes in one piece without peeling paint. Warm it with a hairdryer if needed.
07

Style a Hotel Tray on Your Dresser

A decorative tray on top of your dresser or nightstand corrals the small items that tend to scatter: a perfume bottle, a jewelry dish, a small bud vase, maybe a candle. The tray creates a defined zone that looks intentional even when the rest of the surface is clear. It also makes cleaning simpler. You lift one tray instead of moving five individual objects. Pick one up at any home goods store. It sits on existing furniture and involves zero installation of any kind.

Freestanding tray. Sits on a surface you already own. Zero installation, zero wall contact, zero landlord concern.
08

Upgrade Lampshades (Not Lamps)

Rental apartments often come with generic white drum lampshades on standard floor or table lamps. Swap them for a patterned fabric shade, a pleated style, or a textured linen version that fits the same harp fitting. The lamp stays exactly where it is. Only the shade changes. Pack the original shades in a closet or under the bed and put them back on move-out day. The swap takes under a minute per lamp and adds character to the room without touching anything structural.

The lamp stays. Only the shade swaps. Pack the originals in a closet for move-out day. The rental lamp is unchanged.
