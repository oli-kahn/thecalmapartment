8 Moving-Friendly IKEA Furniture Pieces Every Renter Should Know About

IKEA just designed a whole collection for people who move. Here's what to put on your list.

IKEA's new KOMPISHÄNG collection is the first furniture line explicitly built for renters — every piece assembles without tools, carries like luggage, or mounts with straps instead of screws. Eleven pieces total, launching August 15, 2026. Here are the eight that belong on your radar before they hit stores.

← Back to all rooms
01

No-tools solid pine desk — "two pulls, two clicks"

A solid pine desk that assembles with two pulls and two clicks. That's it. No screws, no hex key, no drilling into anything. It has a built-in carry handle on the side so you can pick it up and walk out the door when the lease ends. The surface is big enough for a laptop, a monitor, and a small lamp — exactly what a rental home office needs without the permanent-desk commitment. You put it together in about 30 seconds. You take it apart just as fast.

Assembles in 30 seconds flat. Disassembles just as fast. Leaves zero marks on anything.
02

Floor-standing red metal bookshelf — no wall mounting needed

A tall red metal bookshelf that stands on the floor and doesn't need wall anchoring. The base is weighted so it won't tip, even on uneven rental floors. Open shelves from both sides — you can place it against a wall or use it as a room divider between a sleeping area and a desk. The red is bright without being loud. When the lease ends, you pick it up and carry it to the next place. No patching, no spackle, no deposit anxiety.

Freestanding. No wall contact. Moves with you — pick it up and go.
03

Over-door hangers with strap attachment — zero screws

Two over-door hangers that hang from a strap draped over the door. No hooks, no screws, no adhesive touching the door surface. One is a canvas pocket version for shoes, accessories, and utensils — the kind of thing that turns a bare closet door into usable storage. The other has open hooks for coats and bags. Both work on any standard interior door and take about five seconds to put up. When you move, you lift the strap off the door and the door looks exactly like it did before.

Hangs from a strap. Takes 5 seconds to install. Removes in 1 second. The door is untouched.
04

Wardrobe organizer worn like a backpack

A hanging wardrobe organizer with integrated backpack straps. You organize folded clothes in the compartments while it hangs from your closet rod. On moving day, you don't empty the drawers into boxes — you take the whole thing down, put the straps over your shoulders, and carry your wardrobe out the door. It's wearable storage that doubles as moving luggage. Zero installation beyond hanging it from the rod that's already in your rental closet.

Wearable furniture. Doubles as moving luggage. Zero installation — hangs from a closet rod.
05

Stacking stools that puzzle-fit into a multi-tier stand

Three solid wood stools that stack and lock together into a vertical stand for plants, books, or bedside essentials. When guests come over, you unstack them into three individual seats. When everyone leaves, you restack them back into a display unit. No hardware anywhere — they fit together like puzzle pieces. For a small rental where every piece of furniture needs to justify its floor space, this is three things in one footprint.

Three pieces of furniture in one. No assembly. Breaks down into portable stools in seconds.
06

Mirror with built-in photo and postcard groove

A freestanding mirror with a groove cut into the frame that holds photos, postcards, and small notes without tape, pins, or adhesive. Lean it against the wall or hang it from an over-door strap — no wall mounting required. The groove is a built-in gallery that you can swap out whenever you feel like refreshing what's on display. It turns a practical object into something personal without putting a single hole in the drywall.

No wall mounting. Leans or straps to a door. The photo groove means zero tape on walls.
07

Plant pot with protective transit cover

A ceramic plant pot that comes with a fitted transit cover. When moving day arrives, you snap the cover on, carry the plant upright to your next apartment, and snap it off. No repotting into a temporary container. No wrapping the plant in a plastic bag and praying it doesn't tip over in the truck. No spilled soil on the moving blanket. The cover is designed into the product from the start — it's not a hack you figure out the night before the movers come.

Designed for moving day. The cover is part of the product, not an afterthought.
08

Rolling under-bed storage bin with carry strap

A flat storage bin on casters that slides under any standard bed frame. During the lease, it holds off-season clothes, extra bedding, or shoes — the stuff you need but don't need to see every day. On moving day, the built-in carry strap turns it from a rolling bin into a portable tote. You roll it out from under the bed, strap it up, and carry it to the truck. No emptying, no repacking, no separate moving boxes.

Lives under the bed during the lease. Becomes luggage when the lease ends.
