8 No-Drill Ways to Hang Art and Decor in a Rental Living Room

Fill your walls with art, mirrors, and personality without a single nail hole.

Most leases say no drilling, which leaves renters staring at bare walls for years. These eight methods hang framed photos, mirrors, shelves, and decorative objects using adhesive, lean-to setups, over-door hooks, and peel-and-stick materials. Everything comes off clean when your lease ends. The walls underneath stay exactly as you found them.

01

Command Picture Hanging Strips for framed art and photos

These velcro-like strips lock together with one side on the frame and one on the wall, holding up to 16 pounds per large pair without a single nail. The strips work best on frames under 10 pounds for large strips, under 5 for medium, and under 3 for small. Lay your gallery arrangement out on the floor first, trace frame positions lightly in pencil, then apply the strips according to the weight of each piece. When you move out, pull the tab straight down slowly and the adhesive releases without peeling paint or leaving residue.

Pull-tab removal, zero tools. The drywall underneath stays smooth and paint-intact.
02

Adhesive mirror tiles for a gallery mirror wall

Stick-on mirror tiles come with peel-and-stick backing and let you build a mirrored accent wall one tile at a time, no drilling or mounting brackets needed. Arrange them in a grid for a clean modern look, or stagger them in an offset pattern for something less predictable. Each tile is lightweight enough that the adhesive holds securely on smooth painted walls. A wall of twelve to sixteen tiles behind a console table or sofa bounces light around the room and makes a small living room feel wider. When you move, heat each tile with a hairdryer and peel it off slowly.

Adhesive backing releases with heat. No brackets, no screws, no patching.
03

Leaning floor art on console tables, mantels, and the floor

Large canvases and framed prints don't need to hang at all. Lean them against the wall on a console table, a low bench, a mantel, or directly on the floor for an instant focal point that touches nothing but the surface it rests on. Layer a tall floor mirror behind a smaller framed piece for depth, or prop two canvases of different sizes side by side. A non-slip pad under the bottom edge keeps pieces from sliding on hardwood or tile. This setup works especially well in corners and next to sofas where a leaned piece reads as intentional rather than unfinished.

Gravity does the work. Nothing touches the wall. Rearrange anytime in seconds.
04

Peel-and-stick wallpaper on one accent wall

Pick a single wall behind the sofa or TV and cover it with removable wallpaper panels that go up like a giant sticker, edge to edge, without paste or water. One accent wall gives you the color or pattern punch of a fully papered room at a fraction of the cost. When your lease is up, you pull from a corner and the whole sheet comes off in one piece, leaving the paint underneath untouched. Smooth walls give the best adhesion. Textured walls can trap air bubbles, so test a sample panel on your specific wall before committing to the full roll.

Pulls off in one piece. No residue, no repainting, no landlord conversation.
05

Washi tape frames drawn directly on the wall

Washi tape is low-tack Japanese masking tape that sticks securely but peels off with your fingers, leaving zero residue even after months on the wall. Run parallel strips to form rectangles around postcards, small prints, or photos for instant no-damage framing. You can also create abstract geometric grids, header stripes, or border lines across a blank stretch of wall. The tape comes in widths from a quarter-inch to two inches, in solid colors and subtle patterns, and costs a few dollars per roll. Change the layout whenever the mood strikes. Pull it off and start fresh in ten minutes.

Peels off with fingers. No heat, no residue, no stress. Costs almost nothing.
06

Over-door wreaths and hanging decor

An over-door hanger hooks onto the top of any standard interior door and gives you a display surface that touches nothing but the door itself. Hang a seasonal wreath, a macrame wall hanging, a lightweight wooden sign, or a fabric banner without drilling into walls or door faces. Most over-door hangers have foam padding on the back so the door face doesn't scratch, and they fit doors up to about 1.75 inches thick. If your living room door leads to a hallway or bedroom, you can use both sides of the door for double the display space. Lift the hanger off and take it with you on move-out day.

Hooks over the door. Touches nothing else. Removes in two seconds.
07

Removable vinyl decals for botanical, abstract, or typographic designs

Vinyl wall decals come in leaf shapes, geometric dots, abstract lines, and typographic quotes, and every one peels off without a trace. You place them by hand and reposition as you go, building a design that fits your exact wall. A scatter of gold dots above a desk, a row of monstera leaves across a blank stretch, or a single large silhouette behind the sofa. Smooth walls give the best adhesion; textured or freshly painted walls need about 30 days of cure time before decals go up. Save the backing sheets so you can re-stick the decals in your next apartment.

Peels off like a sticker. Leaves nothing behind. Reusable if you keep the backing.
08

No-drill floating shelves with adhesive mounting

Small adhesive floating shelves stick directly to the wall with heavy-duty adhesive pads and hold a few paperback books, a trailing plant, or a cluster of candles without brackets or screws. Each shelf supports about 3 to 5 pounds, so keep the load light. Install a pair at staggered heights on either side of a window, or run three in a vertical column in an empty corner. Let the adhesive cure for a full 24 hours before placing anything on the shelf. When it's time to move, heat the adhesive with a hairdryer and peel the shelf off slowly to avoid lifting any paint.

Adhesive releases with gentle heat. The wall underneath stays as-is.
